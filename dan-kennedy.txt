8. proof 
	- the most important tool for exceptional success in selling
	- when you sell, always have an overwhelming quantity and quality of proof
	- testimonials: have a lot of them
	- picture: let them say it for you
	- statistical proof: prove them they don't spend X, but they earn YYY

	- how much proof is enough? too much?
		- never enough

9. k.i.s.s (fred herman)
	- confused clients don't buy
	- fewer options, happy customers
	- you can get rich making the complicated simple
		- present everything in the simplest possible language and in the simplest possible form

10. sell money at a discount
	- prove you offer a compelling return on investment, a bargain
	- you're helping them make money, not losing it
	- sell in your own category of one
	- be able to say: no other ___ will...
	- add bonus and tell their values
	- premiums, guarantees, warranties, club memberships...

11. always compare apples to oranges
	- apple to apple = commodity to the commodity
	- invent an unfair advantage, no play fair bs
	- specialize and customize to prevent apples to apples

12. in search of the free lunch
	- give away free stuff (after sell)
	- don't give away more than your product
	- give away premiums people want for themselves, not business
	- give away thongs people want but rarely buy for themselves
	- the premium should be relevant to your offer or customers
	- create mystery - take a well know concept and give it a cool name
	- "I've got a secret"
	- everything old is new again
	- if it's unknown to someone, it's a secret
	- people want the innovation, but don't want to be the lab rat

14. i'd rather be dumb and persistent than smart and impatient
	- fight the disadvantage of being "too smart"
	- walk away from know-it-all "experts" who gives you all ther reasons you cant succeed
	- persistent can be either good or bad
		1. be very open-minded to testing new, or, for that matter, old ideas and strategies under new conditions
		2. be very quick to abandon those that show little or no promise, regardless of your hopes for them
		3. be patient and persistent with those that do show promise, regardless of anybory else's opinion
	- fail your way toward success, test, test, test

CH.3 START-TO-FINISH STRUCTURE FOR THE SALE
-----------------------
18. the six steps of hte no b.s. sales process
	- interest
	- ability to buy
	- authority
	- predisposition

	- no bs sales process
		1. get permission to sell
		2. design an offer
		3. deliver a structured presentation
		4. use emotional logic
		5. close the sale
		6. morning after

	1. permission to sell
		- "if i could show you how to ___, would you be interested in knowing more about it?"
		- "give me _ minutes, and if I can't show you _ I'll refund your time."
	2. the offer
		- simple, easy to explain, three options
	3. the presentation
		- aida
			- attention
				- briefly outline a set of benefits
			- interest
				- give proof of benefits
			- desire
				- relate the benefits to the prospect
				- discounts incentive
			- action
				- closing
		- pas (problem, agitate, solve) preferable
			- state a problem and secure the prospect's agreement
			- agitate the problem and make it serious
			- "wouldn't it be wonderful if there was..."
	4. emotional logic
		- factors:
			1. love
			2. pride
			3. fear
			4. guilt
			5. greed
		- avoid pain and obtain pleasure
	5. closing the sale
		- yes or yes? today or tomorrow?
	6. the morning after
		- secure client success AFTER the sale
		- write a well written letter where you thank them, contratulate them, restate the resons why the decision was such a good one
		- can send them a gift never discussed upon
		- welcome to our customer family
		- referral, build a system for referrals
			- if after a dozen of clients you still have to prospect, you're doing something wrong

	bonus
		1. stop the sales pitch. start a conversation.
		2. the goal is to discover if the 2 of you are a good fit
		3. when you lose a sale, it's usually at the beginning of the sales process
		4. never chase prospects. instead, get to the truth of whether ther's a fit or not
		5. when prospects offer objections, validate them, and reopen the conversation
		6. never defend yourself or what you have to offer. this only creates more sales pressure
