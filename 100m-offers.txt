
-----------------------
VALUE OFFER: THE VALUE EQUATION
-----------------------
- goal: charge as much as humanly possible
- it's not about "what's fair"
- it's not about cost, it's about value
- whatever the price, it must sound like a steal for your prospect

- the value equation
	- (dream outcome * perceived likelihood of achievement) / (time delay * effort and sacrifice) = value
	1. "what will I make?"
	2. "how will I know it's going to happen?"
	3. "how long will it take?" (answer: instant)
	4. "what is expected of me?" (answer: nothing)

- get the bottom to zero
- perception is reality (logical vs psychological)
	- don't make it cheaper, make fewer of them and charge premium

1. dream outcome (goal = increase)
	- don't create desire, channel it through your offer and monetize
	- people want be
		- perceived as beutiful (more important for women)
		- respected
		- perceived as powerful
		- loved
		- increase their status (one of the most important triggers)
		- making money (more important for men)
	- tip: frame benefits in terms of status gained from the viewpoint of others

2. perceived likelihood of achievement (goal = increase) ... proof
	- people pay for certainty
	- would you be to be a plastic surgeon's 10,000th patient, or their first?
	- proof, what to include in the offer, guarantees, free trial

3. time delay (goal = decrease)
	- long-term outcome
	- short-term experience
	- people buy for the long-term outcome, but stay for the short-term experience
	- we want client to have a big emotional win early (as close as possible to their purchase)
	- give them a fast win
	- fast beat free

4. effort & sacrifice (goal = decrease)
	- other tangible or intangible costs
	- 12 months workout versus 1 day liposuction
	- done for you sevices are more expensive than do it yourself

ENHANCING YOUR OFFER
-----------------------
- the supply and demand curve
	- enhancing your core offer do
		1. increase demand 
		2. decrease perceived supply
	- desire comes from not getting what you want
	- tease demand
		- first whisper that's coming
		- then tease it with some benefits
		- then tell it is launching in a weak
		- then launch it
	- different buyers have different buying threshold
	- demand tends to be fractal
		- 80% buy low level stuff
		- 20% are willing to pay five times the price
	- people will buy easier high ticket stuff when they see those befor
	  them enjoying it (social proof)
	- pent up demand, have fewer slots available than current demand
	@ the longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make

- summary
	- supply and demand are inversely correlated
	- find the sweet spot
	- next
		- use scarcity
		- urgency
		- bonuses
		- guarantees
		- naming
	- FOMO

### SCARCITY
- FOMO
- scarcity (x left / y spots)
- sold out!
- one of the most powerful and least undestood forces to unlock unlimited
  pricing power
- implied demand (people assume that because you are a celebrity you are in
  high demand) so you are expensive
- the rarest the problem, the rarest the solver, the higher the price
- if you ask $50.000 and deliver $500.000 within 3 months, people will pay
- value * rarity = breathtaking profits
- you can't fake "0 fucks given"
- money makes money

- create scarcity
	- you can only handle Y new clients
	- fear of missing out
	- three types of scarcity
		1. limited supply of seats/slots: in general or over X period
of time
		2. limited supply of bonuses
		3. never available again
	- physical products
		- "this month, we are releasing 100 boxes of mint chocolate
		  cookie flavored protein bars"
		1. always sell out
			- use this method once a month
			- do it regurarly
		2. let everyone know that you sold out
	- services
		- it's a little trickier to use scarcity
			1. total business cap - only accepting ... X clients
				- you keep them in with scarcity
				- you create a waiting list for new prospects
				- periodically increase capacity 10-20%
				- works well for highest tiers
			2. growth rate cap - only accepting X clients per week
				- we accempt 5 per week (and 3 spots taken)
				- I can only take 5 calls per day (be patient)
			3. cohort cap - only accepting X client per class
				- x people per period of time
				- works well for high ticket clients
				- have less spot than demand
				- in future everyone rembember you sold out
	- you can use scarcity to even enhance the value of a free lead magnet
		- you can limit the free downlod to only 20 people a week
		- ask them to join a list to be notified when it will be
		  available again
		- make it a desirable thing not everyone has acces to
		- you also will more likely to cosume it when you do get your
		  hands on it
	- honest scarcity (the most ethical scarcity)
