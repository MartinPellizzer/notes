THE CHALLENGER (PT.1)
--------------------------------
1. five types of sales reps
	- the hard worker
	- the relationship builder
	- the lone wolf
	- the reactive problem solver
	- the challenger
2. one clear winner and one clear loser
	- the callenger wins
		- offers the customer unique perspectives
		- has strong two-way communication skills
		- knows the individual customer's value drivers
		- can identify economic drivers of the customer's business
		- is comfortable discussing money 
		- can pressure the customer
	- the challenger can:
		- teach
		- tailor
		- take control
	- the relationship maker fails
3. challengers are the solution selling rep, not just the down economy rep
	- in complex sales, you are asking customers to change their behavior (hard)


--------------------------------
THE CHALLENGER (PT.2) EXPORTING THE MODEL TO THE CORE
--------------------------------
- the challenger use consructive tension
- take control of the customer conversation

1. challengers are made, not born
2. it's the combination of skills that matters (teach, tailor, take control)
3. challenging is about organizational capability, not just rep skills
4. building the challenger sales force is a journey, not an overnight trip

- does the challenger selling model work?
	- teaching for differentiation
		- teach customer something new and valuable to compete in their market
	- tailoring for resonance
	- taking control of the sale
