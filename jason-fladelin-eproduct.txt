2. RESEARCH
	- less is more: people pay more for less (if you give them exactly what they want)
	- outcomes > education
	- desperation: the more desperate, the more likely they buy
	- feelings: make them feel excited about the solution

	- look for tactics, not strategy
		- tactics are things that can be immediately applied
	- find things easy to accetp
		- if people are scared to try something new, they won't do it
	- find things easy to explain
		- omit things that are too hard to grasp
	- focus on what matters
		- don't tell people how to build a clock when all they want is the time of day

	- what matters
		- don't teach intellectually, teach emotionally
		- your job is to spot the info that helps temove the negative emotions and replace them with positive ones
		- reducing, minimizing, eliminating and protecting against
			- embarassment
			- shame
			- fear
			- risk
			- confusion
			- guilt
			- alienation
			- helplessness
			- loneliness
			- overwhelmed
			- rejection
			- lack of self worth
		- increasing, improving, and bettering
			- hope
			- confidence
			- connection
			- clarity
			- acceptance
			- relief
			- understanding
			- fulfillment
			- security
			- peace
			- purpose
			- support
	
	- parkinson's law
		- the most important productivity tool (better than 80/20 rule)
		- "works expands to fill the time available"
		- so put a time limit, or your research forever

	- research done right
		- time limit: 2 solid, straight hours at most to find the best source material to draw from
		- find several dozen articles/videos/reports and other information vehicles on theis subject
		- find the 3-5 vehicles that stand head and shoulders above the rest
		- find these diamonds in the rought in less than 2 hours
	
	- places to look 
		- youtube: look for comments that express appreciation about the outcome
		- long-form articles: 2.000+ words
		- slideshare.net (tip - filetype:pdf, filetype:ppt)
		- forums: sort by most viewd/commented/linked
		- podcast: hard to skim through

	- reserach hack
		- if you find an expert who isn't monetizing, ask interview and get permission to turn it into a product
		- offer to pay them for one hour of their time and offer promotion of the interview
		- always get permission to market their material

	- how to do research
		- evernote, google docs
		- make note of anything you think might be potentially useful
		- once done, eliminate 50% of it
		- then delete another 50%
		- organize what's left into a coherent and cohesive process

	- questions to ask yourself
		- if i only had 6 pages to present my best solution, would this be part of my solution?
		- if i had only 10 minutes to change this person's life, would this information be essential?
		- if i only had 2 hours to create this info product, whould this find its way into the product?
		- does it really matter in the grand scheme of things if someone knows this or not?

	- syntetize 30 year of info into a 1 session training
		- if there are 100 pieces of info that contriubute to the result...
		- ... 80% combined might influence, at most, 1-3% of the outcome...
		- ... another 15% of the rest, will influence 5-10% of the outcome...
		- ... the rest 5% will almost always influence at least 80% of the outcome
		- so find the 5 critical insights that makes all the difference
		- and your product will be better without the rest
		- less is more!!!
		- people will pay you small for the 5% up front
		- and will pay you big for the 95% on the back end

	- the secret is in the research
		- you are basically doing research for your customers
		- instant gratification
		- eliminating or reducing the negative emotions
		- increase the positive emotions
		- easily explained, understood, acted upon

3. OUTLINE THE PRODUCT
-----------------------
	- only 2 ways you can effectively teach something
		- step by step
		- factors

	- step by step
		- 7 minute articles example
			1. open these 3 specific research sites to find 3 different articles on the topic
			2. skim and grab technique to locate your 3 main points for your article
			3. go grab and find 2 sub points to support each main point
			4. use on of the fill in the blank "opening paragraps" templatres for your introduction
			5. introduce your main point. xplain why it's importantn. support it wit two sub points
			6. use one of hte fill in the blanck "conclusion paragraph" templates for your conclusion
			7. proofread your article

	- factors
		- search. find. buy. sourcing physical producat from chhina to sell on amazon
			- lead time
			- minimum order quantity
			- profit margin potential
			- quality control
			- communication

	- when in doubt, go step by step
		- try to keep it less then 11 steps

	- if factors
		- less than 6 major points, each having 3-5 considerations

	- for each step/factor (formula, love formula)
		1. why it's important
		2. what's involved
		3. how to do it
		4. what can happen

	- product introduction
		- keep it short and to the point (1 page)
		1. here's what you should be able to do the first time you go through this product
		2. here's what it can lead to over time if you continue to do it
		3. here are the steps (or factors) involved
		4. here why is these steps are important and why i chose them and put them in this order

	- product conclusion
		- slightly longer than intro
		1. here is haow this can empower you...
		2. here is how this can improve relationships with others around you
		3. here is what you can achieve now, that you might not have been able to before
		4. here is haoe the more you do it the better and easier it will become
		5. here is encouragement to go do it right now

	- product creation summary summary
		1. intro
		2. each step/facto
		3. conclusion
	
	- only 3 factors that motivate everybody
		- power
		- achievement
		- connection

5. CREATE YOUR SALES LETTER
-----------------------
	- short and to the point
	- simple and straight forward
	- created in one sitting

	- for short-form product, short-form copy outperform long-form copy

	- sales letter formula
		1. headline
		2. lead
		3. bullet points
		4. call to action
		5. scarcity

	1. headline
		- write a few ones in less then few minutes and pick the best one
		- unless you know better, use one of the following headlines
			- "how to [benefit] in time without [pain point]"
			- "how to [benefit] in time with [additional specifics]"

	2. lead
		- "for just [$], i'm 100% confident you'll be able to [achieve outcome] immediately after reading/watching/listening to this training. because i'm so confident, if you don't immediately feel you got 10x the value of your $ investment, i'll give you back your money. when you sign up, here's what you'll discover."

	3. bullets
		- master the art of bulletpoint
		- it's the most persuasive thing in copywriting
		- the bullets promise an outcome or benefit without revealing how to get it
		- write as many as possible
		- keep the 10-15 best bulletpoints

		- bullet examples
			- should you _? the answer will shock you.
			- what really matters (and what doesn't) when you _.
			- the # major mistakes people make when trying to _.
			- what you should never do if you want _
			- how to protect yourself from ___
			- how in minuted you can go from ___ to ___
			- the truth about ___
			- the single most important thing to do/avoid when it comes to ___
			- why ___ is better than [normal solution]
			- what holds most people back from ___	
			- the reason why you can't ___ (and what to do about it)
			- a quick way to get over ___ so you can ___
			- a ___ technique to get you ____
			- a weird yet powerful wey to ___
			- a ___ secret that lets you ___
			- # mistake-proof ways to ___
			- a little-known way to ___

	4. call to action
		- "sign up by clicking/tapping on the button below. you'll be take to a form where you imput your payment details. as soon as you fill out and submit the form, you'll be taken to a page where you can instantly download [product name]"
	
	5. scarcity
		- "the price right now is just $# as an introductory special offer. in the very new future the price will increase to reflect the true value. sign up now or you'll have to pay more for this later"

	- why you don't need proof
		- your product is low-priced
		- the sales letter is designed to create an impulsive purchase
		- your outcome is so specific that in asd of itself it is believable

	- if you wish, borrow proof
		- quotes from thought leaders
		- results from the process (not your product)
		- statistics (very powerful)
		- put proof after the lead

	- best practices
		- write in one sitting 
		- optimize it over time (testimonials, tell a story, use a count down timer, etc...)
		- keep look and feel as simple as possible
		- for hosting there are free solutions like wordpress
		- once you make profit, you can invest in your own hosting and web page software
