- you are writing to build a personal brand
- write about tips, tricks, realizations, advice, traps to your past self
- in personal branding, your customer avatar is YOU
- the goal is to solve your own problems
- your personal brand is your USP
- your content over a 6-12 month timeframe is what determines your followers' perception of you

- social leverage
	1. growth
		- is your content being shared?
		- will your content make people click on your profile and wonder "who is this guy?"
		- is your content polarizing due to speaking with confidence on hte subject at hand?
		- study what content do well
	2. authority
		- depth
			- unique perspectives compelling arguments for specific problems
			- long-form content (newsletters and blogs)
		- social proof
			- credentials, titles, niche accomplishments, testimonials
			- your job is to establish authority fast with @depth
	3. authenticity
			- documenting projects
			- expressing opinions, likes, dislikes
			- being vulnerable (but not pitiful)
			- talk about losses and what you plan to do differently

- attention is the new currency

	- free products in exchange for their email

- to get attention
	- calling out who you are talking to
	- targeting a pain point that someone is experiencing
	- opening a curiosity loop
	- using statistics, numbers, and other pattern interrupts that pull readers out of their mindless scrolling

- short form content

- medium form content

- long form content

- foundational tips for holding attention
	- persuasion and storytelling
	- the first step is readability
		- space out your senctences (max 3 sentences) (longer in blog posts)
		- mindful of how your content looks
		- senctences short and impactful
		- if you can't keep them shor, use dashes and parenthesis
		- use bullet points, lists, and other mothods
