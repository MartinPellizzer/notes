six ways to separate prospects from suspects
1. have they told you when they are going to make a decision?
2. have they shared with you a piece of proprietary information?
3. do they have a need you can help them with?
4. are you sure they're the deciosion maker?
	- "how have you made decisions like this in the past?"
	- "is there anyone else who will be involved in making the decision?"
5. do they have financial ability to buy?
	- "when you're making big decisions like this, what criteria do you consider?"
6. has one of your competitors already clearly developed the customer's expectations?
BONUS: ask them to do something little for you at the end of the conversation (es. review documents).

price doesn't belong to prospecting
don't lower price, raise value instead
ask more questions to expand upon their problem

talk about them, not you
your goal on first contact is to ask for a first date:
1. find out one piece of information about the company and/or person with whom you're talking.
2. secure a next step: either an in-person meeting or another phone call at a designated time.
the goal of the first call is to secure the second call

to get first dates:
- referral/connection
- key insight/information
- value statement

referral:
- "hello, Ross, I'm Martin Pellizzer with Ozonogroup. Soluzione ozono found a huge amount of savings through a program I helped them with. would you have time next week for me to come by so i can show you?
	- subject: Pacific Mountain
	- body: Pacific Mountain was able to... (1 line case study). are you in the same spot? feel free to contact me.

key insight
- "hello NAME, this is NAME, with COMPANY, and I have the new IRS regulations that have just come out regarding pensions and how they affect people like you. call me at ...

value statement
- "NAME, i'm NAME with COMPANY. there is a real push by companies to grow their sales by automating the back office. how is your company approaching the back office and its effectiveness?

if they don't reply to one approach try another one, and another one, and another one...

referrals and other major pipeline builders

to ask for referral:
1. be awesome at cutomer care
2. ask at the right moment
3. show appreciation

four steps:
1. ask for referral every time a customer sees value in what you are doing
2. connect with the referral as soon as possible, unless your customer introduced you first
3. keep hte person who gave you the referral in the loop, this encourage him to give you even more referrals
4. show appreciation each step along the way

ask for referrals:
ask for a referral right after a sale.
ask for a referral within a period of time after the sale.
ask every six months
kep it simple:
	- "you mentioned how much you appreciate the way we are helping to automate your systems. I suspect you know other business that would benefit in the same way you do, right?"
	- "how is our new system woeking for you? i'm thinking you are already seeing th value in it. i am sure you know of others who would benefit in the same way. who pops into your mind?"
	- "it's great how we've been able to build a plan that allows your family to achieve the financial goals you've wanted for so long. would you know other familis that would benefit from similar plans?"

connect with the referral
- dont ask your customer to connect you, unless he propose first
- first ask your customer what in particular they feel the referral would like best about what you offer.
- then ask them to connect they way they prefer
- ask for the contact info
- ask the permission to use the customer name (if they dont want to indroduce you personally)
- show appreciation, ask them how you can help

keep your customer up to date
- update you customer about the referral using the communication method they prefer most.

be appreciative each step along the way
- send them a handwritten note, a gift, an incentive of some sort...

use anniversaries (1 year since the started working with you) to ask for referrals.
every six months call and ask for feedback, to sell more, but most importantly to ask for referrals.
these calls are also useful to gather negative feedbacks, which helps improve your product

february/march: ask for referral
july/august: customer feedback
november/december: appreciation

look book for examples

aim to at leas one new lead from each existing customer.
