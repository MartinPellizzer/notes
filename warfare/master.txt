[thread] -----------------------
I just devoured 5 war strategy books in 5 weeks (yes, it was wild).

Here they are 5 lessons I learned:

1. 
Choose your battles wisely.

You can avoid 90% of battles. Those that are high risk and low profit are a waste of your time. Maybe even dangerous. Don't take useless risk. That's a fool's choice.

Suppress your ego and don't draw your sword without a good reason.


2.
You are your worst enemy.

Nothing sabotage you like fear. You may be the strongest against the weakest, but you will lose if you don't believe in yourself. Competence is not enough. You also need confidence.

Have confidence and let your opponent be the one who's scared.

3. 
Be grateful of your enemies.

They give you purpose, strength, and energy. They represent what you despite the most, and so they give you clarity on what to fight for.

Use them as a compass. When you see them go to one direction, you know you have to go to the opposite one.

4.
Back attacks are more dangerous than front attacks.

You can easily dodge a front attack, but hardly a knife in the back. Backstabbers are subtle, elusive, and silent. Learn to smoke them out before they can do serious damage.

Betrayal hurts. So stay alert.

5.
With some enemies, there's no middle ground.

Don't be delusional. You can't please everyone. Some people will declare war to you even if you're the kindest, most honest, and most generous person in the world.

So you must get ready to fight when the time comes, wheter you like conflict or not.
