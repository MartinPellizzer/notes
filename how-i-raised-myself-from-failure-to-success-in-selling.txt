SUMMARY - PART 1

1. ACT ENTHUSIASTIC: force yourself to act enthusiastic, and you'll become enthusisatic. to become enth, act enth.
2. PROSPECTING DAILY: walter lemar talbot: after all. this business of selling narrows down to one thing, just one thing, seeing the people.
3. PUBLIC SPEAKING: to overcome fear and develop courage, join a good course in public speaking.
4. ORGANIZATION: set aside one day as self-organization day.

SUMMARY - PART 2

1. HIT THE TARGET: find what the other fellow wants, then help him find the best way to get there.
2. CREATE DESIRE: when you show a man what he wants, he will move heaven and eath to get it.
3. ASK: cultivate the art of asking questions, not positive statements. inquire rather than attack.
4. FOCUS: find the key issue, the most vulnerable point, then stick to it.
5. WHY: "why" is the most important word in selling.
6. IN ADDITION TO THAT: a man generally has two reasons for doing a thing, one that sounds good, and a real one.
7. LISTEN: be a good listener. give him eager attention and appreciation.

SUMMARY - PART 3

1. DESERVE CONFIDENCE: believe it yourself before expecting others to do so
2. KNOW YOUR BUSINESS: and keep on knowing your business, to have confidence in yourself
3. NO BADMOUTH: speak ill of no man, and peak all the good you know of everybody, praise competitors
4. NEVER EXAGERATE: understate, then exceed expectations ("yes, but I'll know it").
5. TESTIMONIAL: let your witnesses do the talking for you, bring them to the phone
6. LOOK YOUR BEST: choose a character and live it all day long

SUMAMRY - PART 4

1. BE A FRIEND: convince him you are this sincere friend
2. SMILE: honest-to-goodness smile, and you'll be welcome
3. REMEMBER NAMES: impression, repetition, assocaition
4. BE BRIEF: the biggest reason why salesmen lose business is they talk too much
5. ADMIT FEAR: if you are scared, admit it sincerely

SUMMARY - PART 5

1. AIDA: attention, interest, desire, action - save closing points for the close

3. HOW DO YOU LIKE IT?: ask this question after concluding the presentation
