research
	reddit and forums
		check video/slides for keywords (failure, victory, happy, sad, etc...)
	real ass books on subject matter
		amazon, also check questions and reviews
	nbci (national database science): check ingredients, hormones, illnesses, etc...
	sciencedaily.com
	newspapers and industry publications - WSJ, Harvard Business Review, etc.
	existing customers/audience
	calling up enthusiasts, experts, novices
		hey, i'd like to interview you for 15 minutes for some research that i'm doing

prompts
	dimensionalized desire stack (write from the client pov)
		i want ___
		physical image/sensation ___
		what important people/strangers say ___
		deep inside, i feel ___
		consequences ___
		(storytelling, what's in it for me, promises, credibility)
	dimensionalized pain stack (same questions, but negative)
		i want ___
		physical image/sensation ___
		what important people/strangers say ___
		deep inside, i feel ___
		consequences ___
		(problem mechanism, pain agitation, negative lead/close)
		
space and time
	location/geography: where ingredient, process, etc come from?
	historical elements: from history or historical allegory
	contemporary: what is recent and timely? (studies)

	timeframes
		what happens immediately after used product: feeling? instant access?
		7 days after: what negative are going away?
		1 month after: what good things are happening?
		3 months after: what great things are happening?
		6 months to 1 year: how is life never the same?

world and nature
	animals: directly related (indirect, random)

	people: direct, or allegorical
	chemicals/compounds: scientific and lay-names
	foods/spices: scientific and lay-names
	body parts: direct or allegorical
	stars/planets/galaxies: direct or allegorical

	interracing with nature
		what happens when we go with nature: more ease, way it should be, how does product enhance?
		fight with nature: it's harder, not meant to be. why is life without your product fighting against nature?
		flow with nature, not against it

full deck checklist: big idea
	promise (answers "what's in it for me?")
	gripping (suspense, curious, counterintuitive, etc.)
	immediacy (need to know NOW)
	elegance (clear)
	relevant (important to the market)

usp
