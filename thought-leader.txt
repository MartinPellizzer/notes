3 types of thought leader:
	1. activating
		- encourage people to improve their condition
			(conditions that, if not improved, hurt)
		- storytelling (take data and create a story)
		- point out what's at stake if people don't act
		- amplify the voices and experiences of the group you help
		- raise the "belief"
		- highlight how even a small specific action can be impactful
		- define the main issues in novel ways
		- use plain language to simplify the barriers
		- market to people's emotions
	2. systematic
		- redefine the problem
		- prospective shifting content
		- better than what has been done so far
		- explain things through frameworks and mental models (pattern recognition)
		- current systems have failed people
		- redefine things
		- build a mental bridge from where a person is at to a healthier way of thinking
		- share small/large framework
		- give better definitions to vague terms
		- address most common misconceptions
		- tie what you teach to people's previous held beliefs and value
		- give grace and permission slips to people (it's not your fault, society set you up to fail because...)
		- take a stand on pointing out the elephants in the various rooms of your industry
	3. elite service
		- create content for people who are in a position that not many other people are
