PT.1 BEFORE YOU WRITE A WORD
==============================================

what to do before you start writing
	- understanding your business is the hard part
	- writing about it is the easy part
	- you don't need much creativity, you need good swipe files
	- in the land of the blind, the one-eyed man is king
	- there's no problem for which a great sales letter can't be the solution



PT.2 THE KENNEDY SYSTEM
==============================================
a system is a process, formula, framework - not creativity

CH.1 The clay with which to mold
	- STEP 1: get into the customer
		- to persuade someone, you need to understand that person
			1. what keeps them awake at night?
			2. what are they afraid of?
			3. what/who they are angry at?
			4. top 3 frustrations?
			5. what trends are occurring in their business?
			6. what do they secretly, ardently desire most?
			7. build-in bias?
			8. do they have their own language?
			9. who else is selling somenthing similar to them? how?
			10. who else has tried selling them them something similar?
		- how an outsider becomes and insider? (dentist example)
			1. read every industry publication every month
			2. visit websites that host discussions forums for dentists
			3. subscribe to e-mail gourps where only dentists communicate
			4. attend industry functions, conventions, seminars, shows
			5. 'play prospect' with other product and sevice providers
			6. mastermind with dentists and other marketers in industry
		- swim with the current, not against it
	- STEP 2: get into the offer
		- try out the product
		- ask testimonials
		- list feature and benifit, organize by importance
		- look for hidden benefits
	- STEP 3: create a damaging admission and address flaws openly
		- acknowledge the flaws of your product
		- address them as questions, objections, and concerns
		- figure out why they won't respond
			- objections, concerns, fears, doubts, excuses
			- use 3-by-5-inch cards
		- honestly assess the disadvantage of your offer and face them
			- nothing is perfect and everybody knows it
			- openly admit that and you increase credibility

CH.5 HOW TO WRITE SALES PRESSURE
	- all selling is by nature and necessity manipulative
	- must apply pressure to get decision and action
	- STEP 8: motivate action
		- technique #1: intimidation
			- on person to person selling [winning through
			  intimidation book]
			- neediness is repelling, look like you don't need the
			  deal, the client sense your neediness
			1. limited number available
				- if your response is received after our
				  supply is exhaused, it will not be accepted
and your check will be returned uncashed
			2. most will buy
				- band-wagon effect
				- create the idea that a huge trend has
				  developed
				- 'thousands have joined in the last thirty
				  days, so if our phone lines are busy, please
be patient and keep trying'
			3. you will buy only if...
				- challenge their ego and pride
				- 'of course, it takes a very special
				  individual to fully appreciate the value
of...'
			4. you can buy only if...
				- qualify them to buy
				- like triveneto sicurezza
			5. only some can qualify...
				- be part of an elite group
				- the need for approval and recognition
			- with these techniques you reverse buyer/seller roles
			- now the buyer must sell the seller on why he should
			  be permitted to buy
		- techique #2: demonstrate roi - sell money at a discount
			- show them that the proposed purchase actually costs
			  nothing, thanks to the savings or profit it produces
			- selling money at a discount
			- demonstrate roi with case studies
			- [see example book]
		- technique #3: ego appeals
			- ego works like magic
			- portray you solution as status symbol
			- can you afford to appear "behind the times" to ...
			- [book good examples]
		- technique #4: strong guarantee
			- people are more skeptics than ever
			- strong guarantees matter
				1. basic money-back guarantee
				2. refund and keep the premium
				3. redundancy
				4. free trial offer
				5. make the guarantee the primary focus of the
offer
		- technique #5: be a storyteller			
			- study good fiction writers

CH.6 FINALLY, PEN TO PAPER, FINGERS TO KEYBOARD
	- STEP 9: write you first draft
		- just write, no edit grammar or anything else
		- write every persuasive idea that comes to mind
		- then keep what's best in editing phase
	- STEP 10: rewrite for strategy
		- those who are more likely to respond, WILL read a long sales
		  letter
		- write for the buyer, not the nonbuyer
		- real prospects are hungry for information
		- strategic rewriting
			- frustrate the english teacher
				- successful sales letters reads much more
				  like we talk
				- conversational english and popular slang
				- think about a kid talking excitely about a
				  new toy
				- he talks fast, his excitement is infectious
			- increase readership with the double readership path
				- tell them what your're going to tell them
				- tell them
				- tell them again
				- tell them a little differently
				- tell them again, a little more differently
				- tell them what you have told them
				- (try to tell them seven times)
			- tell them your message and promise:
				1. in a straightforward statement
				2. in an example
				3. in a story, sometimes called a 'slice of
life'
				4. in testimonials
				5. in a quote from a customer, expert, or
other spokesperson
				6. in a numbered summary
			- build a 'yes momentum'
				- ask questions
			- tease the reader at the end of each page
				- never end a page with a complete sentence
				- use a teaser blurb (headline like style)	
			- increase readership by improving readability
				- easy to read
				- easy on the eye
				- uses everyday language
				- short paragraphs (3-4 lines)
				- use one line punchy paragraphs too
			- use the first paragraph as an extended headline
			- be entertaining
				- entertaining doesn't mean funny
			- appeal to the senses
				- have them visualize picture and feel the
				  situation with all their senses
			- use big impact words and phrases
				- [examples and books]
			- make your letter reflect your personla style
