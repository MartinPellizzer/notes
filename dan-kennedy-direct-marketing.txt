CH.2 an offer they can't refuse
	- rule #1: there will always be an offer or offers
		- direct marketing imposes discipline
		- you are either disciplined in everything, or you're not disciplined at all
		- the two types of offers
			1. the direct purchase offer
			2. the lead-generation offer
		- the important concept of threshold resistance
			- does your offer fall to low-resistance or high-resistance?
			- examples of low-threshold offers
				- free infoproduct
		- the hybrid approach
		- recap:
			1. the use of offers
			2. the difference between low and high-threshold offers
			3. the use of lead-generation offers
			4. single reason to respond vs. multiple reasons to respond
	- rule #2: there will be a reason to respond right now
		- create urgency, people love procrastination
		- exclusivity. geographic? limited seats? limited resources?
		- scarcity?

CH.3 make them obey orders
	- rule #3: you will give clear instructions
		- give clear direction
		- no confusion
		- anxiety about anything uncertain grows more acute with age
			- add a section "what to expect at your first appointment"
		- the clearer the marching orders, the happier the customer
			- presented with difficult or complex products, customers becomes unhappy	
			- find a way to teach them how to easily use difficult products
		- the power of good direction
			- what happens next?

CH.4 no freeloaders
	- rule #4: there will be tracking, measurement, and accountability
		- vagueness must be banish
		- if you can't measure it, you can't improve it
	- rule #5: only no-cost brand-building
		- never pay for brand-building	
		- un-branded advertising example
			- "warning to mutual fund investors"
			- "expert predicts dramatic change and danger in the next 29 days. this is information you must have - that brokers don't want you to know. for free info ..."
			in this type of ads, don't put your logo or you kill conversion

CH.5 no holes in the bucket
	- rule #6: there will be follow-up
		- every time someone show interest, capture them
			- follow-up on incomming calls
			- follow-up on customer shows and trade shows
			- follow-up on referrals
			- follow-up on new customers (welcome to the family)
			- indifference by provider is the #1 reason customers leave
		- 4-step follow-up campaign:
			1. re-state, re-sell, and extend same offer
				- re-present your offer in the best way possible
			2. stern or humorous "2nd notice" tied to onrushing deadline
				- "are you lost?"
				- "frankly, I'm puzzled, ..."
				- "I'm deeply concerned about your failure to ..."
				- "are you a man or a mouse?"
			3. "third and final notice"
				- 
			4. change the offer
				- different payment terms, bonus, program, solution

CH.6 SHOUTING LOUDER
we are immune to noise
	- rule #7: there will be strong copy
		- attention without relevance and authority is worthless
		- don't be intimidated by criticism, write bold copy
		- the 4 chief sales copy mistakes
			1. sell to their instincts
				- "be the envy of..."
				- dumb copywriter write about:
					1. about the company
					2. products and services
					3. features and benefits
					4. comparative and incremental claim of superiority
					5. price/discounts
					6. guarantee/warranty
				- bonus: line breaks matter, each line is a complete thought
			2. don't write factually and "professionally", but emotionally
			3. don't be timid or blant in your claims and promises
				- timid salesman have skinny kids
			4. aggressive adverising a weak message is a waste of money and vocal cords
