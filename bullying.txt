FREE YOURSELF FROM WORKPLACE BULLYING

become bully-proof and regain control of your life

		- understand the dynamic at the heart of a workplace bullying relationship, so you can prevent it from being created or iterrupt it should it already be in place
		- recognize the self-defeating patterns of thinking and behaviour which you may have adopted as a result of being subject to bullying behaviour

		- recognise why influential people in organisations so often fail to tackle bullying behabiour and thereby enable it

CH.1 what constitutes workplace bullying?
-----------------------

the difference between true bullying and aggressive, non-bullying behaviour

- workplace bullying involves:
	- one-off, frequent or repeated personal attacks which you find emotionally hurtful or professional harmful
	- a deliberate attempt by the bully to undermine your ability to carry out your work, or to injure your reputation, or to undermine your self-esteem and self-confidence
	- a deliberate attempt by the bully to remove personal power from you and keep this power for themselves

----------------------------------------------
self-defeating thoughts and toxic beliefs
the devastating effects of workplace bullying
	- psychologically, emotionally, physically
	- quality of work down

	- don't sleep as well
	- less or more appetite
	- paralyze you
	- you will question if it really wasn't your fault

toxic shame: blaming yourself
	- dont blame yourself
	- dont feeel shame at being targeted
	- you are not responsible for the bully's use of aggressive, coercive behaviour
	- don't burden yourself with a load you cannot be responsible for
	- don't try to help the bully see the error
	- don't try to change the bully
	- no self-blame, it only add suffering and confusion
	- you are not to blame for the actions of the bully
	- your only responsibility is to learn to protect yourself at the time of an attack

	- failing strategies:
		- appealing to the bully's better nature, they have no goodwill
		- try to reason with the bully, your logic argument cant influence their angry emotion
		- trying to appease them, you can't mollify and soothe him
		- feeling sorry for the bully, he doesn't deserve sympathy, they are mistreating you, so you are the one deserving empathy for being targeted
	- you won't:
		- prevent the bully from being aggressive
		- reduce the level of their aggression towards you
		- cause them to change their minds entirely about bullying you
	- hold them accountable for acting abusively
	- they are enemies, not allies
	- don't try to save them, crush them
	- even if you made a mistake at workplace, they have no right to attack you
	- they are not good managers, they are bad bullies
	- two most ineffective responses:
		1. avoid contronting the bully's use of aggression at work
		2. comply with the bully's wishes at the time of an attack

avoiding: a common response to workplace bullying
	- avoiding is a desire to avoid feeling overwhelming levels axiety and fear
	- feel paralysed and disabled when in the presence of person targeting you
	- social embarassment is also a factor
	- you avoid confronting because you don't know how to confront safely
	- but every time you avoid, you give away power
	- everytime you don't strike back, you send them the message they can continue to attack you
	- that you will not put up a stop sign
	- every time you avoid, you give away your power

complying: a second common response to workplace bullying
	- submission is not an effective strategy for getting through a bully
	- your desire to keep some form of connection between you is understandable:
	- but let's make a distincion between:
		1. the intention to preserve a working relationship with a non-bullying, but challenging, colleague
		2. the strategy of compliance or submission towards a workplace bully
	- the first make sense, the second doesn't
	- coercion and fear to control you
	- never submit

CH.4 HOW BULLIES GROOM
why grooming is confusing, how to resist it
----------------------------------------------
what is grooming?
	- grooming is when bullies test the waters too see if you are a valid target
	- lack off assertiveness makes you a good candidate for bullying
	- anyone can be groomed
	- with grooming, they want to evaluate?
		- how you receive their grooming behaviour: robustly, uncomfortably or somewhere in between
		- how effectively you protect your boundaries during the interaction
		- the degree to which you send back the message that you have understood what they are doing and know how to take care of yourself
	- grooming is set of behaviours bullies use to test out which potential targets will be most suceptivle to a campaing of bullying
	- early warning signs:
		- been abusive towards you out of the blue
		- taken advantage of your fear ond confusion to seize control of the interaction
		- used your fear to keep you wrong-goored and self-doubting
		- intimidated you in front of other people in the expectation that no one would intervene on your behalf and you would feel isolated
	- examples

mishandling the moment
	- confusion lead to paralysis, that's why bullies ambush you

what bullies look for when they groom
	- don't recognize the grooming behaviour for what it is
	- feel confused about what just happend
	- feel intimidated and can't say what they would normally say in response to the confusing behaviour or ask the obvious question
	- don't want to face up to the challenging dynamics they find themselves in, so keep quiet when they really need to speak up
	- feel vulnerable because they want to make a good impression, have invested emotionally in a job or friendship, want to be liked or want to make a contribution which will be valued by an authority figure
	- feel uncertain or self-doubting and avoid the controntation they need to have
	- bullies are on the look-out for evidence of confusion or vulnerbility in those they groom

case study 3: groomed
	- [book]
	
solutions:
	- play back the bully patronising words (mirroring), politely but firmly, ending with uptone inflection to denote it's a question
		- this works because:
			1. you change the point of the conversation and get momentum and control
			2. force the bully to find the answer to his own question, throw her off
			3. the bully no longer needs to find an answer to her impertinent question
	
	- he can continue to belittle you, but you send the message that you alone decide what you feel, and where your boundary are
	- colleagues who turn a blind eye to incidents of bullying are colluding with a bullying culture

handling grooming effectively
	- some powerful strategies to protect yourself, preserve your power, and make less likely to be bullied again
		- "I don't see myself like that": you own your self-image
		- "Why you say that?": make them justify, difficult for them
		- "I don't agree.": show you are not afraid of confrontation
		- "I don't uderstand what you said. what do you mean"?

Notes:
	- say: "i don't see myself like that" to challenge and untrue, hurtful and undermining perception of you
	- when you are being groomed it's better to disagree and risk an argument rather than remain silent and risk being bullied
	- say: "what does that mean?" or "i don't know what you just said" to get the issues into the open

putting the issue back to the bully:
	- with these strategies, you may or may not be able to stop a bullying campaign, but you send the message you are not an easy target and that they will struggle a lot if they want to proceed
	- you risk more by going mute than replying back

summary:
	- grooming is testing the waters
	- never stay silent, always reply within 1-2 seconds
	- ask them what they mean?
	- disagree, to show you are not afraid
	- clarify how you see yourself
	- when you feel confused, you act quickly, don't hesitate
	- say something back right at the moment of being perplexed
	- your instinct that something is wrong is spot on
	- show them you know what their trying to achieve
	- even after you defended yourself from grooming, don't relax too much
	- put the issues back to them without being rude or disrespectful
