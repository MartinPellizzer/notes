Introduction
- Ozone has been used commercially in France since 1906 for drinking water treatements.
- increasing demand for minimally processed foods by consumers.
- ozone is preferred to clorine because:
	- clorine dosage is limited by law and at low concentrations it doesn't do much 
	- no chemical residues
	- less potential environmental impact
- legislation is developed in response to the evolving use of ozone:
	- water treatment
	- surface cleaning
	- food produce washes
	- direct food additive
- globally, regulation is still in evolving state
- consult your local regulatory agencies for regulatory constraints
- GRAS (generally recongnized as safe) in 1997
- FDA approves ozone as a secondary firect food addititve, andtimicrobical agent.

History

Ozone regulation
- US:
	- GRAS by EPRI in 1997
	- GRAS by FDA in 2001
-European:
	- ozone used in water treatement since early 1990
	- ozone as antimicrobical agent
	- several advantages over clorine and other chemical disinfectants
	- new legislation IPPC Directive 96/61/EC
	- only irradiation and ionisation have specific regulation by state
	- ozone has no specific legislation, but there are 2 rules:
		1. labelling directive (2000/13) - indicate in the label the type of treatment
		2. novel food regulation (258/97) - premarket authorisation for novel foods and treatments.
	- european council of minister permits the treatment of natural mineral water, but not spring water
	- the SCF (scientific committee for food) opinion is that ozone in mineral water can lead to byproducts.
