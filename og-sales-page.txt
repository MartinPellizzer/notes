- pesticides
	- european commision wants to slash use of chemical pericides by 50%
	  by 2030 (sutainability and biodiverity)
	- farm and fork strategy, make food systems more healthy and
	  environmentally friendly
	- the proposed 50% target would be legally binding for member states
	- chemical pesticides should be replaced by alternatives, with
	  practixes like crop rotation and technologies like precision farming
	- propose to prohibit all pesticide use in sensitive areas like
	  schools, hospitals, parks, and playgrounds (say Frans Timmermans)
	- member states would have to submit regular reports on their progress
	  as part of the new regime.
	- eu founds would also be available over the next five years to cover
	  the cost of any hew requirements
	- many governments already oppose the new proposals
	- Italian MEP herbert dorfmann tells the timing is wrong, given
	  the current food crisis
	- there's another current debate on food security
	- green politicans in the european parliament disagree with this
	  statement
	- par olmgren MEP say that the us needs to set a date for a complete
	  end to the use of chemical pesticides
	- pesticides will give us more problems than benefits in the long run
	- copa cogece, the largest european farmers union, is in favor of
	  reducing the use of chemical pesticides, but not at any cost
	- european farmers and agri-cooperatives need to be provided with
	  cutting-edge scientific tools, available, safe, effective, affordable
	- banning producs without having sufficient effective alternatives in
	  not an efficint approach	
	- the target is to repair the 20% of damage ecosystems by 2030
	- the plans will need approval from the european parliament and
	  european coundil before they can become law
	- to help farmers, memer states must use at least 25% of their cap
	  direct income support budget for echo-schemes
	- and 35% of their rural development budget for actions that benefit
	  the climate and environment
	- the CAP (common agricultural policy)
		- provide safe, healthy adn sustainably produced food
		- earn a stable and fair income
		- protect natural resources, enchance biodiversity, contribute
		  to the fight against climate change
	- in 2023 the new CAP will promote opportunities for sustainable
	  pesticide use, putting agricolture closer to Farm and Fork strategy
