pt.1 the e-myth and american small business
	1. the entrepreneurial myth
		just because you are a good plumber, it doesn't mean you are good at managing a plumbing business
		turning passion into job can actually destroy the passion
		the four stages of every technician suffer from an Entrepreneurial Seizure
			1. exhilaration
			2. terror
			3. exhaustion
			4. despair
	2. the entrepreneurial, the manager, and the techician
		dreams, frets, ruminates
		future, past, present
		dreams, manage, work
	3. infancy: the technician's phase
		owner wants vs business needs
		you are not your business
	4. adolescence: getting some help
		- the boss will always interfere, it's his technician soul
	5. beyond the comfort zone
		- getting small again?
			- if so, you get back to a job, not a business
		- going for broke
			- high-tech, too fast lead to a broke business
		- adolescent survival
			- you either grow or contract
			- write down your plan on paper for you and your employee
			- any plan is better than no plan
			- build a business that works not because of you but without you
	6. maturity and the entrepreneurial prespective
		- fulfill the perceived needs of a specific segment of customers in an innovative way
		- without a clear picture of the customer, no business can succeed
		- every customer is an opportunity to understand what people want
