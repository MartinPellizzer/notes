	1. the entrepreneurial myth
		just because you are a good plumber, it doesn't mean you are good at managing a plumbing business
		turning passion into job can actually destroy the passion
		the four stages of every technician suffer from an Entrepreneurial Seizure
			1. exhilaration
			2. terror
			3. exhaustion
			4. despair
	2. the entrepreneurial, the manager, and the techician
		dreams, frets, ruminates
		future, past, present
		dreams, manage, work
	3. infancy: the technician's phase
		owner wants vs business needs
		you are not your business
	4. adolescence: getting some help
		- the boss will always interfere, it's his technician soul
	5. beyond the comfort zone
		- getting small again?
			- if so, you get back to a job, not a business
		- going for broke
			- high-tech, too fast lead to a broke business
		- adolescent survival
			- you either grow or contract
			- write down your plan on paper for you and your employee
			- any plan is better than no plan
			- build a business that works not because of you but without you
	6. maturity and the entrepreneurial prespective
		- fulfill the perceived needs of a specific segment of customers in an innovative way
		- without a clear picture of the customer, no business can succeed
		- every customer is an opportunity to understand what people want

pt.2 the turn-key revolution: a new view of business
	7. the turn-key revolution
		- systems theory
		- the fanchise phenomenon (macdonalds)
		- turning the key: the business format franchise
		- the true product of a business is the business itself
		- your task is to create a foolproof predictable business
		- a system-dependent business, not a people-dependent business
		- first prototype, then mass produce
	8. the franchise prototype
		- discipline, standardization, and order
	9. working on your business, not in it
		- pretend you're going to franchise your business
		- rules:
			1. the model will provide consistent value to your customers, employees, suppliers, and kenders, beyond what they expect
			2. the model will be operated by people with the lowest possible level of skill
			3. the model will stand out as a place of ipeccable order
			4. all work in the model will be documented in Operations Manuals
				- how to do it guide...
					- what's the purpose of the work?
					- what are the needed steps to do the work?
					- summarize the standards for both the process and the results
			5. the model will provide a uniformly predictable service to the customer
			6. the model will utilize a uniform color, dress, and facilities code

pt.3 building a small business that works
	10. the business development process
		- innovation
			- make a 6-week split test (e.g. 3 weeks brown suit vs 3 weeks blue suit)
		- quantification
			- if you can't measure it, you can't improve it
		- orchestration
			- execute with discipline, no space for guesses
	11. your business development program
		- imagine your business to be the prototype for 5.000 others
		- the Business Development Program is composed of:
			1. your primary aim
			2. your strategic objective
			3. your organizational strategy
			4. your management strategy
			5. your people strategy
			6. your marketing stragegy
			7. your systems strategy
	12. your primary aim
		- work on your life, not in your life
		- "what do I whish my life to look like?"
	13. your strategic objective
		- list of standards
			- money
			- an opportunity worth pursuing
			- who's the customer
			- what's the feeling sold
			- how long to complete the prototype
	14. your organizational strategy
		- organization is hierarchical
		- an organization full of jugglers, is nothing but a circus
		- organization example:
			- president and chief operating officer (COO)
				- vice-president/marketing
					- sales manager
					- advertising/research manager
				- vice-president/operations
					- production manager
					- service manager
					- facilities manager
				- vice-president/finance
					- accounts receivable manager
					- accounts payable manager
		- position contract; commitment and accountability
		- make tree-like diagram
		- look at each position as a franchise prototype of its own
			- if you are the sales manager, you make the Sales Operations Manual
				- the exact scripts for handling incoming calls
				- outgoing calls
				- meeting the customer at the door
	15. your management strategy
		- Operations Manual: nothing but a series of checklists
		- stuff that can be automated, must be automated
	16. your people strategy
		- you cant tell people what to do, but you can engineer an environment that encourage them to do it
		- it's not just a job, it's something more
		- be a great role model and others will follow
		- the work we do is a reflection of who we are
		- you build a structure, and such structure is called a game
		- the rules of the game (example):
			- never figure out what you want your people to do and then try to create a game out of it. if it's to be seen as serious, the game has to come first. what your people do, second
			- never create a game for your people you're unwilling to play yourself. they'll find you out and never let you forget it
			- make sure there are specific ways of winning the game without ending it. the game can never end because the end will take the life right out of your business. but unless there are victories in the process, your people will grow weary. hence, the value of victories now and then. they keep people in the game and make the game appealing, even when it's not
			- change the game from time to time. the tactics, not the strategy. the strategy is its ethic, the moral underpinning of your game's logic. this must remain scarosanct, for it is the foundation of you and your people's commitment to each other. but change is necessary. for any game can become ordianry, no matter how exhilarating it may be at the beginning
		- the logic of the game
			- give them purpose, values, worthwhile standards, sense of relationship, 	
			- give them a place to call home
			- a place where they can live organized
		- playing th game
			- have a well planned process
			- a documented system

		- make promises no one else dare to make, and fulfill them 100%
