pt.1 the e-myth and american small business
	1. the entrepreneurial myth
		just because you are a good plumber, it doesn't mean you are good at managing a plumbing business
		turning passion into job can actually destroy the passion
		the four stages of every technician suffer from an Entrepreneurial Seizure
			1. exhilaration
			2. terror
			3. exhaustion
			4. despair
	2. the entrepreneurial, the manager, and the techician
		dreams, frets, ruminates
		future, past, present
		dreams, manage, work
	3. infancy: the technician's phase
		owner wants vs business needs
		you are not your business
	4. adolescence: getting some help
		- the boss will always interfere, it's his technician soul
	5. beyond the comfort zone
		- getting small again?
			- if so, you get back to a job, not a business
		- going for broke
			- high-tech, too fast lead to a broke business
		- adolescent survival
			- you either grow or contract
			- write down your plan on paper for you and your employee
			- any plan is better than no plan
			- build a business that works not because of you but without you
	6. maturity and the entrepreneurial prespective
		- fulfill the perceived needs of a specific segment of customers in an innovative way
		- without a clear picture of the customer, no business can succeed
		- every customer is an opportunity to understand what people want

pt.2 the turn-key revolution: a new view of business
	7. the turn-key revolution
		- systems theory
		- the fanchise phenomenon (macdonalds)
		- turning the key: the business format franchise
		- the true product of a business is the business itself
		- your task is to create a foolproof predictable business
		- a system-dependent business, not a people-dependent business
		- first prototype, then mass produce
	8. the franchise prototype
		- discipline, standardization, and order
	9. working on your business, not in it
		- pretend you're going to franchise your business
		- rules:
			1. the model will provide consistent value to your customers, employees, suppliers, and kenders, beyond what they expect
			2. the model will be operated by people with the lowest possible level of skill
			3. the model will stand out as a place of ipeccable order
			4. all work in the model will be documented in Operations Manuals
				- how to do it guide...
					- what's the purpose of the work?
					- what are the needed steps to do the work?
					- summarize the standards for both the process and the results
			5. the model will provide a uniformly predictable service to the customer
			6. the model will utilize a uniform color, dress, and facilities code

pt.3 building a small business that works
	10. the business development process
		- innovation
			- make a 6-week split test (e.g. 3 weeks brown suit vs 3 weeks blue suit)
		- quantification
			- if you can't measure it, you can't improve it
		- orchestration
			- execute with discipline, no space for guesses

			3. your organizational strategy
			4. your management strategy
			5. your people strategy
