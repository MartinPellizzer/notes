SUMMARY: SELLNG IN THE NEW ECONOMY
----------------------------------------------

1. Immunity to "NO": never angry, never defensive, always patient
	1. determine you are going to exert control
	2. determine you will get positive results under negative situations
	3. get you ego out ofthe way
	4. do not confuse refusal with rejection
	5. focus on achieving positive results
	6. understand that most no's are erroneous
	7. ingore the "nos", acknowledge but never accept
	8. respond only to real objections, not fake ones

2. Preparation: positive power of negative preparation (black-box)
	1. forget preconceived labels of "positive" or "negative"
	2. make a list of every question, concern, or objection to address
	3. make a lista of everything that could go wrong
	4. develop positive responses to all the negatives
	5. have your information, ideas, and documentation well organized
	6. take great confidence from your thorough preparation

3. Listen: people are desperate for attention
	- you can exchange attention for dollars
	- listen a lot more, talk a lot less
	- make a list of what you are listening to 
	- grade yourself of how many points of the list you've aquired info

4. Avoid Contamination: seniority and laziness is toxic
	- only listen to people that are at the top of their game

5. Personal Packaging: image management
	- as the packaging of products is important, so is packaging of people
	- 80% of impression is based on nonverbal input
	- for every 1 person who says it, there are 10,000 who think it
	- would you rather be right or rich?
	- people prefer dealing with successful people
	- when you are dressed well, you get more respect
	- navy blue blazer, grey wool slacks, blue dress shirt, you pick the tie

6. Remember The Goal: always be selling
	- timid salespeople have skinny kids
	- you are doing it for the money, not applause

7. Do expectations govern results?
	- you get what you expect 
	- super-successful salespeople expect successful results
	- maxwell maltz: theater of the mind

8. Proof: always have overwhelming proofs
	- testimonials
	- pictures
	- statistics

3. K.I.S.S.: keep it stupid simple!
4. Bargain: they pay $ to get $$$
5. Unicorn: specialize and customize
6. Free: give away free stuff after sale
7. Patience: test, keep what's work, trash the rest

CH.3 START-TO-FINISH STRUCTURE FOR THE SALE
-----------------------
18. the six steps of hte no b.s. sales process
	- interest
	- ability to buy
	- authority
	- predisposition

	- no bs sales process
		1. get permission to sell
		2. design an offer
		3. deliver a structured presentation
		4. use emotional logic
		5. close the sale
		6. morning after

	1. permission to sell
		- "if i could show you how to ___, would you be interested in knowing more about it?"
		- "give me _ minutes, and if I can't show you _ I'll refund your time."
	2. the offer
		- simple, easy to explain, three options
	3. the presentation
		- aida
			- attention
				- briefly outline a set of benefits
			- interest
				- give proof of benefits
			- desire
				- relate the benefits to the prospect
				- discounts incentive
			- action
				- closing
		- pas (problem, agitate, solve) preferable
			- state a problem and secure the prospect's agreement
			- agitate the problem and make it serious
			- "wouldn't it be wonderful if there was..."
	4. emotional logic
		- factors:
			1. love
			2. pride
			3. fear
			4. guilt
			5. greed
		- avoid pain and obtain pleasure
	5. closing the sale
		- yes or yes? today or tomorrow?
	6. the morning after
		- secure client success AFTER the sale
		- write a well written letter where you thank them, contratulate them, restate the resons why the decision was such a good one
		- can send them a gift never discussed upon
		- welcome to our customer family
		- referral, build a system for referrals
			- if after a dozen of clients you still have to prospect, you're doing something wrong

	bonus
		1. stop the sales pitch. start a conversation.
		2. the goal is to discover if the 2 of you are a good fit
		3. when you lose a sale, it's usually at the beginning of the sales process
		4. never chase prospects. instead, get to the truth of whether ther's a fit or not
		5. when prospects offer objections, validate them, and reopen the conversation
		6. never defend yourself or what you have to offer. this only creates more sales pressure

			- followed by a faxed invitation to a group teleseminar
	2. hanging out wiht losers

		- no coffee break, chit-chats

		- have a system for everything, leads, follow-up, presentation, referrals
	6. poor self-discipline
