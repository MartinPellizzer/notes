-----------------------
WRITING THAT WORKS
-----------------------
- most people don't know how to write
- stop the "smart talk"

write down:
	1. what you want the reader to do
	2. then the 3 most important things the reader needs to understand before taking action
	3. finally start writing your message

people who write well do well
	- busy people dont have time to waste
	- good writing speeds things up

making time to write well
	- more != better
	- honestly, you only have 2-3 hours a day you can use productively
	- effective communication it the ultimate time saver

- don't mumble - and other principles of effective writing
	- above all, don't mumble
		- decide what to say, then say it loud and clear

	- make the organization of your writing clear
		- people write badly because they cannot think clearly.
		- lead with the reason you are writing
		- make an outline of your mahor points
		- then fill in the details
		- then add conclusion (if you need one)
		- then end with a summary
		- very lengthy documents start with a summary

	- use short paragraphs, short sentences, and short words
		- put into practice
		- examples
			- now > currently
			- start > initiate
			- show > indicate
			- finish > finalize
			- speed up > expedite
			- use > utilize
			- place, put > position
		- distill your thinking to its essence, before opening your mouth
		- clarity is the ability to express your deepest emotions in the simplest words

	- make your writing active and personal
		- choose the active voice over the passive one
			- it is recommended > we recommend
			- he should be told > get Alice to tell him

	4. avoid vague adjectives and adverbs
		- very overspent > overspent by $10,000
		- slightly behind schedule > one day late
		- avoid cliches:
			- very good
			- great success
			- awfully nice
			- richly deserved
			- basically accurate
			- vitally important
		- use instead
			- instantly accepted
			- tiny raise
			- rudely turned down
			- moist handshake
			- short meeting
			- tiresome speech
			- crisp presentation
			- black coffee
			- baffling instructions
			- lucid recommendation
	
5. use down-to-earth language
	- jargon arises more out of fear than arrogance
	- don't use show off fad words or vague abstractions
	- buzzword bing
	- write the way you talk
	- big words make you look small
	
	- examples
		- to interface >> discuss, meet, worth with
		- to impact modality >> to affect, to do, to style, method
		- resource constrained >> not enough people (or money)

	- be clear and direct, people will appreciate you more, not less

6. be specific
	- use numbers to communicate what you want to tell

7. choose the right word
	- know the precise meaning of every word you use
	- [see list of examples]

8. make it perfect
	- no typos, misspellings, errors in numbers or dates

9. come to the point
	- i'm writing a long letter because i don't have the time to write a short one

10. write simply and naturally - the way (we hope) you talk
	- written language and spoken one should be the same
	- your writing should sound just like you

11. strike out words you don't need
	- at this point in time >> now

12. use current standard english

13. don't write like a lawyer or a bureaucrat
	- there's no excuse to write like a lawer

14. keep in mind what your reader doesn't know
	- never expect people to read your mind.

15. punctuate carefully
	- don't use wuotation marks for emphasis

16. understate rather than overstate
	- never exaaggerate and deceive
	- understating is more persuasive
	- half-truths are bad news
	- don't overdo it

17. write so that you cannot be misunderstood

18. use plain english even on technical subjects
