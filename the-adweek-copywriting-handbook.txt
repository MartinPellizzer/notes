1. general knowledge -------------------------------------------------------
the best copywriters have a variety of interests and master many skills
thirst for knowledge

2. specific knowledge -----------------------
you must become an expert of the product to really become effective
you must know your customer
you must understand a product nature (presentation)
scare tactics don't usually work (statistics)

3. practice, practice, practice -----------------------
copywrinting is simply a written form of communicating facts and emotions
the process of having thoughts organized in your mind and transferring them onto paper
first draft, then polish it

4. the purpose of all the graphic elements of an ad -----------------------
(see image template)
1. headline
2. subheadline
3. photo or drawing
4. caption
5. copy
6. paragraph headings
7. logo
8. price
9. response device
10. overall layout
the only goal of the headline/subheadline is to make the reader read the first sentence of the copy

5. the first sentenct -----------------------
make the first sentence short
"it had to happen"
magazines use this technique
the goal of the first sentence is to read the second sentence, and so on

[6. creating the perfect buying environment] -----------------------
your copy has to put the prospect into a relaxed buying environment
ex. sell products at a discount with big types and lots of busy graphic
ex. if expensive, present and evironment that show class and refinement, that exuded confidence and trust
you control the environment with graphic but especially words

[7. resonating with the reader] -----------------------
get the customer to nod in the affirmative and agree with you
get your reader to say "yes"
harmony is the key
speak in the consumer language

[8. the slippery slide] -----------------------
your readers should be so compelled to read your copy that they cannot stop reading until they read all of it as if sliding down a slippery slide
start with a story or a news

[9. assumed constraints] -----------------------
you never really know

[10. seeds of curiosity] -----------------------
more traffic = more sales -> more readership = more traffic
at the end of a paragraph, put a very short sentence that offers some reason for the reader to read the next
ex. but there's more. so read on. but i didn't stop there. let me explain. new here comes the good part. and here's why.

[11. copy as emotion] -----------------------
1. every word has an emotion associated with it and tells a story
2. every good ad is an emotional outpouring of words, feelings and impressions
3. you sell on emotion, but you justify a purchase with logic
logic often doesn't work
braindump all your emotions and feeling in the first draft
every word has an emotion attached

[12. selling the concept, not the product] -----------------------
*** never sell a product or service. always sell a concept
big idea, usp, gimmick
use drama


[13. the incubator process] -----------------------
*** take a break from your work and do something pleasurable while your brain incubates
keep the creative stuff separate from the rest of the company

[14. how much copy should you write?] -----------------------
the higher the pricee, the more copy required
unusual items require more time to sell
longer-copy: create an environment, buying mood, tell the full story
*** copy should be long enough to cause the reader to take the action you requested

[15. the art of personal communication] -----------------------
*** every advertisement should be a personal message from the advertiser to the prospect
use words like I, you, me
use yoru name in a byline

[16. the copy sequence] -----------------------
copy must flow, grammatically and logically
leading the reader
(read april example)
make sentences like small blocks, then put them in a flowchart to make them flow 
ex. interest excitement > drama > why different > how to play > unique features > justify purchase > lasting play value > service > ask for order
flowing in a logical sequence (like face-to-face person)
answer question before readers start asking them
problem restatements
list several big ideas
use others ads to "pattern"

[17. the editing process] -----------------------
in the editing process, you refine your copy to express exactly what you want to express with the fewest words
- look for any "that" words
- edit for rhythm
- consider combining sentences
- eliminate unnecessary words
- rearange thoughts so they flow better
put your copy away for a day, come back and discover new stuff
correct all errors
