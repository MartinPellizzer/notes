SPIN

1. Situation Questions:
	- "How long have you had your present equipment?"
	- "Could you tell me about your company's growth plans?"

2. Problem Questions:
	- "Is this operation difficult to perform?"
	- "Are you worried about the quality you get from your old machine?"

3. Implication Questions:
	- "How will this problem affect your future profitability?"
	- "What effect does this reject rate have on customer satisfaction?"

4. Need Questions:
	- "Would it be useful to speed this operation by 10 percent?"
	- "If we could import the quality of this operation, how would that help you?"

CLOSING
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Investigate more, don't rush, build needs

2. Answer all questions before CTA, ask them for concerns:

	- ME: Well, I think that covers everything, Mr. X. But before we go further, could I check whether there are any areas that you feel I should tell you more about?
	- BUYER: Yes, you haven't mentioned...
	- ME: Then let mecover that now. They way it works...

3. Summatize benefits

4. Proposing commitment - don't ask, propose
	- ME: Then I might suggest that the most logical next step would be for you and your accountant to come and see one of these systems in operation.

	2. summarize benefits
	3. propose commitment

INVESTIGATING
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ask questions to uncover Implied Needs (problems) and develop them into Explicit Needs (actions).

SITUATION QUESTIONS: The fewer the better. Only the neccesary ones (3-5).
	- "What's your position?"
	- "How long have you been here?"
	- "Do you make the purchasing decisions?"
	- "What do you see as your objectives in this area?"

	- "What sort of business do you run?"
	- "Is it growing or shrinking?"
	- "What's your annual sales volume?"
	- "How many people do you employ?"

	- "What equipment are you using at present?"
	- "How long have you had it?"
	- "Is it purchased or leased?"
	- "How many people use it?"

Avoid:
	- "Tell me about your business."
	- "What steps do you go through in making a purchasing decision here?"


PROBLEM QUESTIONS: Better than situation questions, but still not good enough (Implied Needs).
	- "Are you satisfied with your present equipment?"
	- "What are the disadvantages of the way you're handling this now?"
	- "Isn't it difficult to process peak loads with your present system?"
	- "Does this old machine give you reliability problems?"


IMPLICATION QUESTIONS: Develop from PQ, talk the language of decision makers.
					   (Explicit Needs LV.1).
	- "What effect does that have on output?"
	- "Could that lead to increased costs?"
	- "Will it slow down your proposed expansion?"

NEED QUESTIONS: (Explicit Needs LV.2)
	- Develop from IQ.
	- Focus on solutions and not on problems.
	- The customer tells YOU the benefits.
	- NQ reduce objections.
	- Great for internal sales (in HT, an average of 5.4 buyers are involved per sale).


	- "Is it important to you to solve this problem?"
	- "Why would you find this solutions so useful?"
	- "Is there any other way this could help you?"

	- "How do you think a faster machine would help you?"

	- "How would that help?"
	- "Why is it important to solve this problem?"
	- "What benefits do you see?"
