-----------------------
HEADLINES
-----------------------

basis:
	clear, not clever
	make a promise
	specific (to a problem, to an audience)
	take a stance (polarizing)
	tease the ending, without revealing the answer

checklist:
	1. how many (2, 3)
	2. the approach (tips, mistakes)
	3. the what (saving money in your 20s - 3 levels deep)
	4. the who (project managers, real estate agents)
	5. the why (benefits or outcomes)
	6. twist the knife (and... more benefits and outcomes)

tip: 
	write the full long headline first with all the elements in the list,
	then you can always shorten it if it feels "too long"

templates:
	1. X little known [something] that could be causing your [problem]
	2. a day in the life of a [profession]
	3. how [industry] is taking advantage of [audience] - and how to fix it
	4. is [thing] a scam? here's my honest review
	5. how to get rid of [somethign] forever, even if you've tried everything
	6. a step-by-step guide for X
	7. how to make a ton of money [doing something unconventional]
	8. the secret to achieving [something desirable]
	9. 9 out of every 10 people [make this mistake]. are you one of them?
	10. ... (written by someone in the industry)
	11. how to survive your first [meaningful experience]
	12. what [someone credible] can teach us about [topic]
	13. the ultimate guide to [something]
	14. X lessons learned [from doing something]
	15. why something happens (according to science)
	16. X things [this detail] say about you
	17. a guaranteed method for [achieving something / avoiding something]
	18. how to achieve [something] without needing to give up [someting]
	19. how to [achieve something] in [a short amount of time]
	20. the X things most people don't know about [topic]
	21. the X biggest mistakes people make when [doing something]
	22. the X best books to read if you're interested in [topic]
	23. X tips for [overcoming some obstacles]
	24. the best ways to get [something] without needing [something]
	25. want [someting]? try [this]

formatting:
	title
	credibility introduction
	main points
	conclusion

before writing, summarize your idea:
	what's the working title?
	what's 1 thing you can tell the reader (right away) to make them trust you (credibility)?
	what are the main points?
	what's the final takeaway?

3 small but powerful formatting techniques:
	6 single-sentence openers
		open with 1 strong, declarative sentence (ex. oneliner tweet)
		open with a thought-provoking question (ex. did you know...)
		open with a controversial opinion
		open with a moment in time (ex. in 1982, at 5:46, when I was 17 years old)
		open with a vilnerable statement (ex. i've never been)
		open with a weird, unique inight
	turn main points into subheads
		your subheads should tell a story
		separate the page into thirds
		bold the main point
		then write inside the main point section
	1/3/1 writing rhythms

twitter bio framework:
	I write about [topic]. [credibility]

linkedin bio framework:
	get people to explore "what else"

rules for twitter:
	minimum of 2x short-form posts per day
	target 3-5x short-form posts per day, 7 days per week - 2+ long-form posts per week
	cadence:
		3 to 4 hours between posts, from 8:30am to 10pm
		alernate short and long form. 
	text + media is the golden combination

rules for linkedin:
	more posting != faster growth
	post 1x per day
	post each morning at 8:30am
	post:
		long-form text + media (picture or video)
		short-form text + carousels
	make sure your lead-in "hook" is visible in the first 5 lines (before the "see more" button)
